## Field of Real Numbers

$\mathbb{R}$: Set of real numbers

A set is a collection, possibly empty, of elements

$a,b\in \mathbb{R}$: Pair of real numbers

Read “$a,b$ are in $\mathbb{R}$” or “$a,b$ are elements of $\mathbb{R}$”


**Fact:** Can add, subtract, or multiply any $a,b\in \mathbb{R}$ to get another $c\in \mathbb{R}$, and:

$$
\begin{align*}
a+b &= b+a \\
a-b &= -(b-a) \\
ab &= ba
\end{align*}
$$


Can also divide $b$ by $a$ if $a\neq 0$

Say “$\mathbb{R}$ is a field”

## A Linear Equation

**Claim**:
The equation $4x=2$ is satisfied for exactly one $x\in \mathbb{R}$

It is easy to verify $x=\frac{1}{2}$ satisfies $4x=2$

Use division to show $x=\frac{2}{4} =\frac{1}{2}$ is the only solution

---

**Fact**:
If $a,b\in \mathbb{R}$ and $a\neq 0$, then $ax=b$ for exactly one $x\in \mathbb{R}$

**Proof**:
Divide both sides by $a$ (or multiply by $\frac{1}{a}$) to get $x=\frac{a}{a} x=\frac{b}{a}$

This works because $\mathbb{R}$ is a field and division undoes multiplication

**Definition**:
A vector is an arrow with a tail (initial point) and head (terminal point)

**Example**:
Standard Position

$O=( 0,0)$: The origin

**Definition**:
A vector $v$ is in standard position iff $v=\overrightarrow{OA}$ for some $A$

The phrase “$P$ iff $Q$” means “$P$ is true exactly when $Q$ is”

**Example**:

$v$ and $w$ are in standard position

$w\prime $ is not in standard position

## Comparing Vectors

**Definition**:
Two plane vectors are equal iff they have the same “direction” and “length”

**Example**:
In the previous example, $w=w\prime $ but $w\neq v$

**Fact**:
Vectors in standard position are equal iff they have the same entries

**Fact**:
Every vector is equal to a unique vector in standard position
